The Artistic Process

The process of creating artistic photographs involves more than just pointing a camera and clicking. It requires a deep understanding of light, composition, and the subject. For Jennifer Heyne Hanson, this process begins with a connection to the subject, whether it’s a person, a landscape, or an object. She seeks to understand the essence of what she is photographing, aiming to capture not just the visual elements but the emotional and psychological depth as well.

The Role of Lighting

Lighting plays a crucial role in Jennifer Heyne Hanson’s artistic photography. She often manipulates natural and artificial light sources to create dramatic effects, mood, and ambiance in her images. The use of light is not just technical but also emotional, as it helps in conveying the mood and tone of the story being told.

Composition and Visual Elements

Composition is another key element in Jennifer Heyne Hanson’s photography. She carefully considers the placement of subjects within the frame, the use of negative space, and the balance of colors and textures. Each element is chosen for its ability to contribute to the narrative, creating a visually appealing and engaging image.
