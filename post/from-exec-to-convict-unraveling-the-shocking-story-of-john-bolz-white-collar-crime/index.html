Understanding White Collar Crime

So, what exactly is white collar crime? The term was first coined in the 1930s by sociologist Edwin Hardin Sutherland, who defined it as “crime committed by a person of respectability and high social status in the course of their occupation.” White collar crime can take many forms, including embezzlement, fraud, insider trading, and money laundering. These crimes are often characterized by their complexity and the fact that they are committed by individuals who are in a position of trust.
